  • The GAMMA Research Group

    The Group for Applied Mathematical Modeling & Analytics (GAMMA) is a research group that studies the use of mathematical modeling, operations research, and analytics to solve problems that arise from a wide variety of practical and theoretical applications. The tools and techniques developed by the group have been used to solve problems in the areas of vehicle routing, transportation, data association, network interdiction, social network analysis, and operations management.

    The Group for Applied Mathematicla Modeling & Analytics (GAMMA) is a research group that studies the use of mathematical modeling, operations research, and analytics to solve problems that arise from a wide variety of practical and theoretical applications. The tools and techniques developed by the group have been used to solve problems in the areas of vehicle routing, transportation, data association, network interdiction, social network analysis, and operations management.

  • Mission and Vision

    We aim to develop solutions for complex problems via the effective use of operations research and artificial intelligence techniques. By doing so, GAMMA provides its students with an extensive learning experience that prepares them for future research endeavors. Our vision is to consolidate the research developments and become a recognized research hub by the operations research community.

GAMMA Seminar Series: Mixed Integer Bilevel Optimization with k-optimal Follower: A Hierarchy of Bounds

On April 7, 2021, Dr. Oleg Prokopyev will present his research on bilevel mixed integer optimization problems. In particular, this work focuses on a Stackelberg game where the follower is not completely rational, and may not solve their problem to optimality. Dr. Prokopyev is a Professor in the Department of Industrial Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh. For information on how to join this seminar, click "Learn More".

GAMMA Graduate: Cai Gao defends his thesis - On Routing Unmanned Aerial Vehicles for Surveillance and Reconnaissance Activities

In his dissertation, Cai Gao describes two important UAV routing problems: risk and reward asset routing problem (R2ARP) which and the close-enough traveling salesmen problem (CETSP). The solution approaches for these problems featured a mix of exact cutting plane approaches for MILP and MINLP, as well as an efficient Lin-Kernighan heuristic for larger instances.

GAMMA Graduate: Luca Wrabetz completes M.S., next chapter at University of Pittsburgh

Upon being conferred his M.S. with concentration in Operations Research from UB's ISE department, Luca is moving to the University of Pittsburgh to pursue a Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering. Here, he will be advised by Dr. Oleg Prokopyev, an academic "brother" of Dr. Walteros.
