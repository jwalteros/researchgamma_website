  • The Gamma Research Group

    GAMMA is a research group that studies the use of mathematical modeling, operations research, and analytics to solve problems that arise from a wide variety of practical and theoretical applications. The tools and techniques developed by the group have been used to solve problems in the areas of vehicle routing, transportation, data association, network interdiction, social network analysis, and operations management.

  • The Gamma Research Group

    GAMMA is a research group that studies the use of mathematical modeling, operations research, and analytics to solve problems that arise from a wide variety of practical and theoretical applications. The tools and techniques developed by the group have been used to solve problems in the areas of vehicle routing, transportation, data association, network interdiction, social network analysis, and operations management.

  • Mission and Vision

    We aim to develop solutions for complex problems via the effective use of operations research and artificial intelligence techniques. By doing so, GAMMA provides its students with an extensive learning experience that prepares them for future research endeavors. Our vision is to consolidate the research developments and become a recognized research hub by the operations research community.

Ningji Wei is defending his thesis – Integer Programming Methods for Boolean Interdiction Games

Interdiction games are commonly used to model scenarios where two actors with conflicting objectives both aim to operate optimally. Ningji defined an abstract game called the Boolean interdiction that captures the essence of an important subclass of such interdiction games and developed several theoretical tools to solve them.
